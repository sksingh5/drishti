## Key Findings Summary

1. **DiCRA occupies a unique niche** — no other platform combines India-specific, district-level, agriculture-focused, open-source climate intelligence with NABARD institutional backing and development finance linkage
2. **The biggest gap is decision support** — DiCRA shows data but doesn't help users decide what to do with it
3. **The highest-leverage expansion is a Climate Risk API** — every Indian bank needs climate risk data for RBI/TCFD compliance; DiCRA + NABARD are uniquely positioned
4. **The Climate Stack vision is the right direction** — positioning DiCRA as India's agricultural climate data layer (like Aadhaar for identity, UPI for payments)
5. **Digital Earth Africa is the architectural model** — similar mission, more mature infrastructure, developer sandbox approach worth emulating
6. **Key threat: Krishi DSS expansion** — the closest competitor could expand into climate resilience, but DiCRA's DPG status and financial sector access are differentiators
7. **GEE is the engine, DiCRA is the car** — DiCRA's value is in curation, context, and accessibility, not raw computing (which GEE already provides)
